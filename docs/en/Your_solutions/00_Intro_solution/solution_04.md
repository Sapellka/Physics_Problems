# Task 04 – Rearranging the Pendulum Period Formula

## Problem Statement

The period of a simple pendulum is given by

$$
T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}
$$

Rearrange the formula to obtain an expression for the gravitational acceleration $g$.

## Theory

Algebraic rearrangement of formulas involves isolating the desired variable through inverse operations.  

The main steps include:

- removing square roots by squaring
- isolating the variable by multiplication or division

## Step-by-Step Solution

Start from

$$
T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}
$$

Divide both sides by $2\pi$

$$
\frac{T}{2\pi} = \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}
$$

Square both sides

$$
\left(\frac{T}{2\pi}\right)^2 = \frac{L}{g}
$$

Rearrange to isolate $g$

$$
g = \frac{L}{\left(\frac{T}{2\pi}\right)^2}
$$

Simplify the denominator

$$
g = \frac{L}{\frac{T^2}{4\pi^2}}
$$

Multiply by the reciprocal

$$
g = \frac{4\pi^2 L}{T^2}
$$

## Final Result

$$
g = \frac{4\pi^2 L}{T^2}
$$

## Interpretation

The gravitational acceleration can be determined experimentally by measuring the pendulum length and its oscillation period. This relationship forms the basis of classical pendulum experiments used to measure $g$.
