# Task 08 – Definite Integral of Sine

## Problem Statement

Compute the area under the curve

$$
f(x) = \sin x
$$

between

$$
x = 0
$$

and

$$
x = \pi
$$

## Theory

The area under a curve is given by a definite integral

$$
A = \int_a^b f(x)\,dx
$$

The antiderivative of $\sin x$ is

$$
\int \sin x\,dx = -\cos x
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Evaluate the integral

$$
A = \int_0^{\pi} \sin x \, dx
$$

Find the antiderivative

$$
-\cos x
$$

Evaluate at the limits

$$
A = [-\cos x]_0^{\pi}
$$

Substitute values

$$
A = -\cos(\pi) + \cos(0)
$$

Using

$$
\cos(\pi) = -1
$$

and

$$
\cos(0) = 1
$$

$$
A = -(-1) + 1
$$

$$
A = 2
$$

## Final Result

$$
A = 2
$$

## Interpretation

The positive area arises because the sine function remains above the x-axis within the interval from $0$ to $\pi$.
