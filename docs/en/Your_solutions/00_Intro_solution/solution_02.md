# Task 02 – Systems of Equations

## Problem Statement

Solve the system

$$
2x + 3y = 12
$$

$$
x - y = 1
$$

## Theory

A system of linear equations can be solved using substitution.  
One variable is expressed from one equation and substituted into the other.

## Step-by-Step Solution

From the second equation

$$
x - y = 1
$$

Solve for $x$

$$
x = 1 + y
$$

Substitute into the first equation

$$
2(1+y) + 3y = 12
$$

Expand

$$
2 + 2y + 3y = 12
$$

Combine terms

$$
2 + 5y = 12
$$

Solve for $y$

$$
5y = 10
$$

$$
y = 2
$$

Substitute back

$$
x = 1 + 2
$$

$$
x = 3
$$

## Final Result

$$
x = 3, \quad y = 2
$$

## Interpretation

The solution represents the intersection point of two lines in the $xy$ plane.
