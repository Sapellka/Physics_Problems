# Task 05 – Trigonometric Components of a Vector

## Problem Statement

A vector has magnitude

$$
A = 15
$$

and forms an angle

$$
\theta = 60^\circ
$$

with the horizontal axis.

Determine the horizontal and vertical components.

## Theory

For a vector with magnitude $A$ and angle $\theta$ relative to the horizontal axis, the components are

$$
A_x = A \cos\theta
$$

$$
A_y = A \sin\theta
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Horizontal Component

$$
A_x = 15 \cos 60^\circ
$$

Using

$$
\cos 60^\circ = \frac{1}{2}
$$

$$
A_x = 15 \times \frac{1}{2}
$$

$$
A_x = 7.5
$$

### Vertical Component

$$
A_y = 15 \sin 60^\circ
$$

Using

$$
\sin 60^\circ = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}
$$

$$
A_y = 15 \times \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}
$$

$$
A_y = \frac{15\sqrt{3}}{2}
$$

Approximate value

$$
A_y \approx 12.99
$$

## Final Result

Horizontal component

$$
A_x = 7.5
$$

Vertical component

$$
A_y = \frac{15\sqrt{3}}{2} \approx 13.0
$$

## Interpretation

Vector decomposition allows motion and forces to be analyzed independently along orthogonal axes, which simplifies many problems in mechanics.
