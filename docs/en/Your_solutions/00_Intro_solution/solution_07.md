# Task 07 – Logic and Series (Fly Problem)

## Problem Statement

A bicycle is located 10 meters from a wall and moves toward it at a constant speed of

$$
1 \text{ m/s}
$$

A fly travels between the bicycle and the wall at

$$
2 \text{ m/s}
$$

Each time it reaches the wall or bicycle it instantly reverses direction.

Determine the total distance traveled by the fly before the bicycle reaches the wall.

## Theory

Instead of summing an infinite sequence of flights, the problem can be simplified by determining the total time before the collision occurs.

The fly moves continuously during this time.

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Time Until Collision

The bicycle travels a distance

$$
d = 10 \text{ m}
$$

at a speed

$$
v = 1 \text{ m/s}
$$

Time required

$$
t = \frac{d}{v}
$$

$$
t = \frac{10}{1}
$$

$$
t = 10 \text{ s}
$$

### Fly Distance

The fly travels at

$$
v_f = 2 \text{ m/s}
$$

for the entire duration.

Distance traveled

$$
d_f = v_f t
$$

$$
d_f = 2 \times 10
$$

$$
d_f = 20
$$

## Final Result

Total distance traveled by the fly

$$
d_f = 20 \text{ m}
$$

## Interpretation

Although the fly performs infinitely many trips, the total distance is finite because the motion occurs within a finite time interval.
