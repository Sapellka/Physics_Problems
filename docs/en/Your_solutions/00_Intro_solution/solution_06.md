# Task 06 – Function Analysis

## Problem Statement

Consider the function

$$
f(x) = 3x^2 - 12x + 7
$$

Determine any local maxima or minima.

## Theory

For a differentiable function, local extrema occur where the first derivative is zero.

Conditions:

1. $f'(x) = 0$ identifies critical points
2. The second derivative determines whether the point is a maximum or minimum

## Step-by-Step Solution

### First Derivative

Differentiate the function

$$
f'(x) = \frac{d}{dx}(3x^2 - 12x + 7)
$$

$$
f'(x) = 6x - 12
$$

### Critical Point

Set the derivative equal to zero

$$
6x - 12 = 0
$$

$$
6x = 12
$$

$$
x = 2
$$

### Second Derivative

$$
f''(x) = 6
$$

Since

$$
f''(x) > 0
$$

the function is concave upward.

### Value of the Function

Evaluate at $x = 2$

$$
f(2) = 3(2)^2 - 12(2) + 7
$$

$$
f(2) = 12 - 24 + 7
$$

$$
f(2) = -5
$$

## Final Result

The function has a minimum at

$$
(x, f(x)) = (2, -5)
$$

## Interpretation

Because the quadratic coefficient is positive, the parabola opens upward. Therefore the critical point corresponds to a minimum.
