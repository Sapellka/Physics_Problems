# Task 03 – Proportionality in the Law of Gravitation

## Problem Statement

The gravitational force is

$$
F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}
$$

Determine how the force changes if:

- distance $r$ doubles
- both masses are halved

## Theory

The gravitational force is proportional to

$$
F \propto \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}
$$

Changes can be analyzed through scaling factors.

## Step-by-Step Solution

Let the original force be

$$
F_0 = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}
$$

### Change in Mass

Both masses are halved

$$
m_1' = \frac{m_1}{2}, \quad m_2' = \frac{m_2}{2}
$$

Product of masses

$$
m_1' m_2' = \frac{m_1 m_2}{4}
$$

### Change in Distance

Distance doubles

$$
r' = 2r
$$

Squared distance

$$
r'^2 = 4r^2
$$

### New Force

$$
F' = G \frac{m_1 m_2 /4}{4r^2}
$$

$$
F' = \frac{1}{16} G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}
$$

## Final Result

$$
F' = \frac{F_0}{16}
$$

## Interpretation

The gravitational force becomes **16 times smaller** due to the combined effect of reduced masses and increased distance.
