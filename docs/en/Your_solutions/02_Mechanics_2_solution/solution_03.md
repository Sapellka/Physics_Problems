# Task 03 – Conservation of Energy in a Pendulum

## Problem Statement

A pendulum of length

$$
L = 1.0 \text{ m}
$$

is released from an initial angle of

$$
15^\circ
$$

Determine the speed of the bob at the bottom of the swing.

## Theory

If air resistance and friction are neglected, mechanical energy is conserved.

The loss of gravitational potential energy is converted into kinetic energy:

$$
mgh = \frac{1}{2} mv^2
$$

For a pendulum released from angle $\theta_0$, the vertical drop is

$$
h = L(1 - \cos \theta_0)
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

The initial height difference relative to the lowest point is

$$
h = L(1 - \cos 15^\circ)
$$

With

$$
L = 1.0 \text{ m}
$$

one gets

$$
h = 1 - \cos 15^\circ
$$

Using

$$
\cos 15^\circ \approx 0.9659
$$

gives

$$
h \approx 0.0341 \text{ m}
$$

Now apply energy conservation:

$$
mgh = \frac{1}{2} mv^2
$$

Cancel the mass:

$$
gh = \frac{1}{2} v^2
$$

So,

$$
v = \sqrt{2gh}
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
v = \sqrt{2 \cdot 9.81 \cdot 0.0341}
$$

$$
v \approx 0.818 \text{ m/s}
$$

## Final Result

The speed of the pendulum bob at the bottom is

$$
v \approx 0.82 \text{ m/s}
$$

## Interpretation

The pendulum starts with gravitational potential energy and converts it into kinetic energy as it descends. Because the initial angle is small, the height change is modest and the resulting speed is less than $1 \text{ m/s}$.
