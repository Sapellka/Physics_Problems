# Task 05 – Inelastic Collision of a Runner and a Cart

## Problem Statement

A runner of mass

$$
70 \text{ kg}
$$

moves at

$$
3 \text{ m/s}
$$

and jumps onto a stationary cart of mass

$$
140 \text{ kg}
$$

Determine:

- the final speed of the cart and runner,
- whether kinetic energy is conserved.

## Theory

In a perfectly inelastic collision, the objects stick together after impact. Linear momentum is conserved:

$$
m_1 v_1 + m_2 v_2 = (m_1 + m_2) v_f
$$

Kinetic energy is generally not conserved in such a collision.

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Final speed

Here,

$$
m_1 = 70, \qquad v_1 = 3
$$

$$
m_2 = 140, \qquad v_2 = 0
$$

Use momentum conservation:

$$
70 \cdot 3 + 140 \cdot 0 = (70 + 140) v_f
$$

$$
210 = 210 v_f
$$

$$
v_f = 1 \text{ m/s}
$$

### 2. Check kinetic energy

Initial kinetic energy:

$$
K_i = \frac{1}{2} \cdot 70 \cdot 3^2
$$

$$
K_i = 35 \cdot 9
$$

$$
K_i = 315 \text{ J}
$$

Final kinetic energy:

$$
K_f = \frac{1}{2} \cdot 210 \cdot 1^2
$$

$$
K_f = 105 \text{ J}
$$

Since

$$
K_f \neq K_i
$$

kinetic energy is not conserved.

## Final Result

The final speed is

$$
v_f = 1.0 \text{ m/s}
$$

Kinetic energy is not conserved:

$$
K_i = 315 \text{ J}, \qquad K_f = 105 \text{ J}
$$

## Interpretation

Momentum is conserved because the collision time is short and external horizontal forces are negligible. Kinetic energy decreases because some of it is transformed into heat, sound, and internal deformation during the impact.
