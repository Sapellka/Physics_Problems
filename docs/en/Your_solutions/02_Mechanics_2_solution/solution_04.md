# Task 04 – Energy and Momentum in a Collision

## Problem Statement

A block of mass

$$
m_1 = 0.5 \text{ kg}
$$

slides down a frictionless track from height

$$
h = 3.0 \text{ m}
$$

At the bottom it collides and sticks to a second block of mass

$$
m_2 = 1.5 \text{ kg}
$$

initially at rest.

Determine the speed of the combined mass immediately after the collision.

## Theory

Before the collision, the first block converts gravitational potential energy into kinetic energy:

$$
m_1 g h = \frac{1}{2} m_1 v_1^2
$$

During the sticking collision, linear momentum is conserved:

$$
m_1 v_1 = (m_1 + m_2) v_f
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Speed of the first block at the bottom

From energy conservation,

$$
m_1 g h = \frac{1}{2} m_1 v_1^2
$$

Cancel the mass:

$$
gh = \frac{1}{2} v_1^2
$$

Thus,

$$
v_1 = \sqrt{2gh}
$$

Substitute

$$
g = 9.81 \text{ m/s}^2, \qquad h = 3.0 \text{ m}
$$

Then

$$
v_1 = \sqrt{2 \cdot 9.81 \cdot 3.0}
$$

$$
v_1 \approx 7.67 \text{ m/s}
$$

### 2. Speed after the collision

Use momentum conservation:

$$
m_1 v_1 = (m_1 + m_2) v_f
$$

Therefore,

$$
v_f = \frac{m_1 v_1}{m_1 + m_2}
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
v_f = \frac{0.5 \cdot 7.67}{0.5 + 1.5}
$$

$$
v_f = \frac{3.835}{2.0}
$$

$$
v_f \approx 1.92 \text{ m/s}
$$

## Final Result

The speed of the combined mass just after the collision is

$$
v_f \approx 1.92 \text{ m/s}
$$

## Interpretation

The block gains speed during the descent because the track is frictionless. The collision is perfectly inelastic, so momentum is conserved but kinetic energy is not. The final speed is much smaller because the moving mass must carry the second block as well.
