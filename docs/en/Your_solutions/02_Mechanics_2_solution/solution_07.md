# Task 07 – Dynamics with Friction

## Problem Statement

A block of mass

$$
5 \text{ kg}
$$

is placed on top of a block of mass

$$
10 \text{ kg}
$$

A horizontal force of

$$
45 \text{ N}
$$

is applied to the $10$ kg block. The $5$ kg block is tied to the wall. The coefficient of kinetic friction between all moving surfaces is

$$
\mu_k = 0.2
$$

Determine the acceleration of the $10$ kg block.

## Theory

When the lower block moves, two kinetic friction forces oppose its motion:

- friction from the top block,
- friction from the ground.

For kinetic friction,

$$
f_k = \mu_k N
$$

Newton's second law for the lower block is

$$
\sum F_x = m a
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Let the $10$ kg block move to the right.

### 1. Friction from the top block

The upper block is tied to the wall, so the lower block slides relative to it. The normal force between them is

$$
N_1 = 5g
$$

Therefore, the friction magnitude is

$$
f_1 = \mu_k N_1 = 0.2 \cdot 5 \cdot 9.81
$$

$$
f_1 = 9.81 \text{ N}
$$

This force acts to the left on the lower block.

### 2. Friction from the ground

The ground supports the weight of both blocks, so the normal force is

$$
N_2 = (10 + 5)g = 15g
$$

Hence,

$$
f_2 = \mu_k N_2 = 0.2 \cdot 15 \cdot 9.81
$$

$$
f_2 = 29.43 \text{ N}
$$

This also acts to the left.

### 3. Net force and acceleration

The net horizontal force on the $10$ kg block is

$$
F_{\text{net}} = 45 - 9.81 - 29.43
$$

$$
F_{\text{net}} = 5.76 \text{ N}
$$

Now apply Newton's second law:

$$
a = \frac{F_{\text{net}}}{10}
$$

$$
a = \frac{5.76}{10}
$$

$$
a = 0.576 \text{ m/s}^2
$$

## Final Result

The acceleration of the $10$ kg block is

$$
a \approx 0.58 \text{ m/s}^2
$$

to the right.

## Interpretation

The applied force is largely canceled by friction from the top block and the ground. The remaining net force is small, so the resulting acceleration is also small.
