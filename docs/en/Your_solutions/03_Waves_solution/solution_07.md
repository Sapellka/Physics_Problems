# Task 07 – Standing Wave Modes

## Problem Statement

A standing wave with four antinodes is produced on a string of length

$$
L = 80 \text{ cm} = 0.80 \text{ m}
$$

Determine the wavelength.

## Theory

For a string fixed at both ends, the allowed standing-wave modes satisfy

$$
L = n \frac{\lambda}{2}
$$

where $n$ is the harmonic number.

The number of antinodes equals $n$.

## Step-by-Step Solution

Four antinodes means

$$
n = 4
$$

Use

$$
L = n \frac{\lambda}{2}
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
0.80 = 4 \frac{\lambda}{2}
$$

This simplifies to

$$
0.80 = 2\lambda
$$

Therefore,

$$
\lambda = \frac{0.80}{2} = 0.40 \text{ m}
$$

## Final Result

The wavelength is

$$
\lambda = 0.40 \text{ m}
$$

or

$$
40 \text{ cm}
$$

## Interpretation

A larger number of antinodes means a higher harmonic and therefore a shorter wavelength on the same string.
