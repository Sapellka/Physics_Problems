# Task 02 – String Harmonics

## Problem Statement

A guitar string has length

$$
L = 64 \text{ cm} = 0.64 \text{ m}
$$

Its fundamental frequency is

$$
f_1 = 330 \text{ Hz}
$$

Determine the speed of the wave on the string.

## Theory

For the fundamental mode of a string fixed at both ends, the length of the string is half a wavelength:

$$
L = \frac{\lambda}{2}
$$

Thus,

$$
\lambda = 2L
$$

The wave speed is then

$$
v = f \lambda
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

For the fundamental mode,

$$
\lambda = 2L = 2(0.64) = 1.28 \text{ m}
$$

Now use

$$
v = f \lambda
$$

Substitute the data:

$$
v = 330 \cdot 1.28
$$

$$
v = 422.4 \text{ m/s}
$$

## Final Result

The wave speed on the string is

$$
v = 422.4 \text{ m/s}
$$

## Interpretation

The fundamental mode is the lowest allowed standing-wave pattern on the string. Because the wavelength is twice the string length, the wave speed follows directly from the measured fundamental frequency.
