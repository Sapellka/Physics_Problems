# Task 01 – Wave Properties

## Problem Statement

A sound wave in air has frequency

$$
f = 440 \text{ Hz}
$$

The speed of sound is:

- in air: $343 \text{ m/s}$,
- in water: $1482 \text{ m/s}$.

Determine the wavelength in air and in water.

## Theory

For any wave,

$$
v = f \lambda
$$

Therefore,

$$
\lambda = \frac{v}{f}
$$

If the frequency stays the same and the wave enters another medium, the wavelength changes because the wave speed changes.

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Wavelength in air

Use

$$
\lambda_{\text{air}} = \frac{v_{\text{air}}}{f}
$$

Substitute the data:

$$
\lambda_{\text{air}} = \frac{343}{440}
$$

$$
\lambda_{\text{air}} \approx 0.780 \text{ m}
$$

### 2. Wavelength in water

Use

$$
\lambda_{\text{water}} = \frac{v_{\text{water}}}{f}
$$

Substitute the data:

$$
\lambda_{\text{water}} = \frac{1482}{440}
$$

$$
\lambda_{\text{water}} \approx 3.37 \text{ m}
$$

## Final Result

The wavelength in air is

$$
\lambda_{\text{air}} \approx 0.780 \text{ m}
$$

The wavelength in water is

$$
\lambda_{\text{water}} \approx 3.37 \text{ m}
$$

## Interpretation

The frequency is set by the source and does not change when the wave passes into a different medium. Since sound travels much faster in water than in air, the wavelength in water is much longer.
