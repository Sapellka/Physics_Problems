# Task 05 – Echo Ranging

## Problem Statement

A person shouts toward a cliff and hears the echo $1$ second later.

The speed of sound in air is

$$
v = 343 \text{ m/s}
$$

Determine the distance to the cliff.

## Theory

An echo involves a round trip:

- from the person to the cliff,
- from the cliff back to the person.

Thus the total travel distance is twice the actual cliff distance.

The relation is

$$
\text{distance} = \text{speed} \times \text{time}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

The total distance traveled by the sound is

$$
d_{\text{total}} = vt = 343 \cdot 1 = 343 \text{ m}
$$

Since this is the round-trip distance, the one-way distance is

$$
d = \frac{343}{2}
$$

$$
d = 171.5 \text{ m}
$$

## Final Result

The cliff is

$$
171.5 \text{ m}
$$

away.

## Interpretation

The sound wave travels to the cliff and back before it is heard as an echo. Therefore the actual distance is half of the total sound-travel distance.
