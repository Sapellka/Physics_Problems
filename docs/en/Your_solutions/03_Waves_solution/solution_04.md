# Task 04 – Phase Difference

## Problem Statement

Find the phase difference between two points on a wave separated by a distance

$$
\frac{\lambda}{3}
$$

Express the answer in radians.

## Theory

The phase difference associated with spatial separation $\Delta x$ is

$$
\Delta \phi = k \Delta x
$$

where

$$
k = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda}\Delta x
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

The separation is

$$
\Delta x = \frac{\lambda}{3}
$$

Use

$$
\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda} \cdot \frac{\lambda}{3}
$$

The wavelength cancels:

$$
\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{3}
$$

## Final Result

The phase difference is

$$
\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{3} \text{ rad}
$$

## Interpretation

A separation of one full wavelength corresponds to a phase shift of $2\pi$. Therefore, one third of a wavelength corresponds to one third of $2\pi$, which is $\frac{2\pi}{3}$.
