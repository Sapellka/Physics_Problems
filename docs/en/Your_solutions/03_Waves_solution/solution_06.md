# Task 06 – Wave Equation Parameters

## Problem Statement

A wave is described by

$$
y(x,t) = 0.05 \sin(2\pi x - 50\pi t)
$$

where $x$ and $y$ are in meters and $t$ is in seconds.

Determine:

- amplitude $A$,
- wavelength $\lambda$,
- frequency $f$,
- wave speed $v$.

## Theory

The standard form of a traveling wave is

$$
y(x,t) = A \sin(kx - \omega t)
$$

where

- $A$ is amplitude,
- $k$ is wave number,
- $\omega$ is angular frequency.

The relations are

$$
k = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda}
$$

$$
\omega = 2\pi f
$$

$$
v = \frac{\omega}{k} = f\lambda
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Compare

$$
y(x,t) = 0.05 \sin(2\pi x - 50\pi t)
$$

with

$$
y(x,t) = A \sin(kx - \omega t)
$$

This gives

$$
A = 0.05 \text{ m}
$$

$$
k = 2\pi
$$

$$
\omega = 50\pi
$$

### 1. Amplitude

$$
A = 0.05 \text{ m}
$$

### 2. Wavelength

Use

$$
\lambda = \frac{2\pi}{k}
$$

Thus,

$$
\lambda = \frac{2\pi}{2\pi} = 1 \text{ m}
$$

### 3. Frequency

Use

$$
f = \frac{\omega}{2\pi}
$$

Hence,

$$
f = \frac{50\pi}{2\pi} = 25 \text{ Hz}
$$

### 4. Wave speed

Use

$$
v = f\lambda
$$

So,

$$
v = 25 \cdot 1 = 25 \text{ m/s}
$$

## Final Result

The wave parameters are:

$$
A = 0.05 \text{ m}
$$

$$
\lambda = 1 \text{ m}
$$

$$
f = 25 \text{ Hz}
$$

$$
v = 25 \text{ m/s}
$$

## Interpretation

The numerical coefficients in the sine argument determine the spatial and temporal oscillation rates. The amplitude controls the maximum displacement, while the wavelength and frequency together determine the propagation speed.
