# Task 02 – Range Optimization

## Problem Statement

Show that the range is maximized at $\theta = 45^\circ$.

## Theory

Range formula:

$$
R(\theta) = \frac{v_0^2 \sin(2\theta)}{g}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Maximize $R(\theta)$:

$$
\frac{dR}{d\theta} \propto \frac{d}{d\theta} \sin(2\theta)
$$

$$
\frac{d}{d\theta} \sin(2\theta) = 2\cos(2\theta)
$$

Set to zero:

$$
\cos(2\theta) = 0
$$

$$
2\theta = 90^\circ
$$

$$
\theta = 45^\circ
$$

## Final Result

Maximum range occurs at:

$$
\theta = 45^\circ
$$

## Interpretation

The sine function reaches its maximum at $90^\circ$, giving optimal energy distribution between vertical and horizontal motion.
