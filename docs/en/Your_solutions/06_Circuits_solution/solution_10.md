# Task 10 – Average Current of a Lightning Bolt

## Problem Statement

A lightning bolt transfers charge

$$
Q = 30 \text{ C}
$$

in time

$$
\Delta t = 2 \text{ ms} = 0.002 \text{ s}
$$

Determine the average current.

## Theory

Average current is defined by

$$
I_{\text{avg}} = \frac{\Delta Q}{\Delta t}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Substitute the values:

$$
I_{\text{avg}} = \frac{30}{0.002}
$$

$$
I_{\text{avg}} = 15000 \text{ A}
$$

## Final Result

The average current is

$$
I_{\text{avg}} = 1.5 \times 10^4 \text{ A}
$$

## Interpretation

Lightning transfers a large amount of charge in a very short time, so the resulting current is extremely large.
