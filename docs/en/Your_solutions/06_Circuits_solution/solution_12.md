# Task 12 – Transformer Voltages and Currents

## Problem Statement

A transformer has

$$
N_p = 1000
$$

turns on the primary coil and

$$
N_s = 200
$$

turns on the secondary coil.

The primary voltage is

$$
V_p = 120 \text{ V}
$$

and the secondary current is

$$
I_s = 3 \text{ A}
$$

Determine:

- the secondary voltage,
- the primary current.

## Theory

For an ideal transformer,

$$
\frac{V_s}{V_p} = \frac{N_s}{N_p}
$$

and power is conserved:

$$
V_p I_p = V_s I_s
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Secondary voltage

$$
V_s = V_p \frac{N_s}{N_p}
$$

$$
V_s = 120 \cdot \frac{200}{1000}
$$

$$
V_s = 24 \text{ V}
$$

### 2. Primary current

Use power conservation:

$$
I_p = \frac{V_s I_s}{V_p}
$$

$$
I_p = \frac{24 \cdot 3}{120}
$$

$$
I_p = 0.6 \text{ A}
$$

## Final Result

The secondary voltage is

$$
V_s = 24 \text{ V}
$$

The primary current is

$$
I_p = 0.6 \text{ A}
$$

## Interpretation

Because the transformer steps the voltage down by a factor of 5, it steps the current up by a factor of 5 in the opposite direction of the voltage ratio.
