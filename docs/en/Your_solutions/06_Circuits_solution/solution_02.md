# Task 02 – All Equivalent Resistances from Three 1 Ohm Resistors

## Problem Statement

Using exactly three resistors of

$$
1\,\Omega
$$

each, determine all possible unique equivalent resistances that can be formed.

## Theory

With three identical resistors, the distinct combinations come from series, parallel, and mixed series-parallel arrangements.

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. All three in series

$$
R_{\text{eq}} = 1 + 1 + 1 = 3\,\Omega
$$

### 2. All three in parallel

$$
\frac{1}{R_{\text{eq}}} = 1 + 1 + 1 = 3
$$

$$
R_{\text{eq}} = \frac{1}{3}\,\Omega
$$

### 3. Two in series, then in parallel with the third

Two in series give

$$
2\,\Omega
$$

Then

$$
R_{\text{eq}} = \frac{(2)(1)}{2+1} = \frac{2}{3}\,\Omega
$$

### 4. Two in parallel, then in series with the third

Two in parallel give

$$
R = \frac{1 \cdot 1}{1+1} = \frac{1}{2}\,\Omega
$$

Then

$$
R_{\text{eq}} = 1 + \frac{1}{2} = \frac{3}{2}\,\Omega
$$

## Final Result

The unique equivalent resistances are

$$
3\,\Omega
$$

$$
\frac{3}{2}\,\Omega
$$

$$
\frac{2}{3}\,\Omega
$$

$$
\frac{1}{3}\,\Omega
$$

## Interpretation

These four values are the only distinct results when all three identical resistors are used exactly once.
