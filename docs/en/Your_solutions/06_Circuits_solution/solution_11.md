# Task 11 – Power and Energy in a Resistor

## Problem Statement

A resistor of

$$
R = 100\,\Omega
$$

has voltage

$$
V = 50 \text{ V}
$$

applied across it.

Determine:

- the power dissipated,
- the energy consumed in 5 minutes.

## Theory

For a resistor,

$$
P = \frac{V^2}{R}
$$

Energy consumed over time is

$$
E = Pt
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Power

$$
P = \frac{50^2}{100}
$$

$$
P = \frac{2500}{100} = 25 \text{ W}
$$

### 2. Energy in 5 minutes

Convert time:

$$
t = 5 \text{ min} = 300 \text{ s}
$$

Then

$$
E = Pt = 25 \cdot 300
$$

$$
E = 7500 \text{ J}
$$

## Final Result

The power dissipated is

$$
P = 25 \text{ W}
$$

The energy consumed in 5 minutes is

$$
E = 7500 \text{ J}
$$

## Interpretation

Power gives the rate of energy dissipation, while energy is the total amount dissipated over a chosen time interval.
