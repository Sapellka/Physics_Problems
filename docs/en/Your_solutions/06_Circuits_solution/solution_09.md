# Task 09 – Current from Charge as a Function of Time

## Problem Statement

The charge flowing through a wire is

$$
Q(t) = 5t^2 + 5
$$

Determine the current at

$$
t = 3 \text{ s}
$$

## Theory

Current is the time derivative of charge:

$$
I(t) = \frac{dQ}{dt}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Differentiate:

$$
I(t) = \frac{d}{dt}(5t^2 + 5) = 10t
$$

Now substitute

$$
t = 3
$$

$$
I(3) = 10 \cdot 3 = 30 \text{ A}
$$

## Final Result

The current at

$$
t = 3 \text{ s}
$$

is

$$
I = 30 \text{ A}
$$

## Interpretation

Because the charge grows quadratically with time, the current grows linearly with time.
